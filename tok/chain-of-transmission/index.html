Look back through the queue. There was never a button for “is it true.” There never was one. This tool can tell you a report was faithfully carried to you across the centuries — it cannot tell you the thing first said was true of the world.

That is not a fault in the tool. It is the boundary of what this kind of validation reaches. The chain certifies transmission. The matn-check certifies agreement with the canon. Neither one is a person checking the claim against the world — and that limit is built into the method, not a gap a better tool would close.

The four questions — religion's answers

You just filled three cells

The same four questions you ran on science now have answers on the religion side — and they look different.

V — How is a claim validated?
By the soundness of its chain (unbroken, every carrier upright and precise), graded صحيح / حسن / ضعيف, plus a check that the text agrees with the established canon. Validation certifies faithful transmission — not a reader's own verification.
C — Who is the authority / who carries it?
The accredited chain of carriers — whose individual reliability is itself judged by an expert science (عِلْم الرِّجال, the “science of men”). Authority is spread across the carriers and the scholars who grade them — never in a lone reader.
R — What can the method not reach?
The truth of the content itself. The procedure reaches transmission and canon-agreement; the world the claim is about lies past its edge.
