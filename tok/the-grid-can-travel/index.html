I

Caesar crossed the Rubicon

Rome · January 49 BC

A small river marked the northern boundary of Italy. No general could cross it under arms without declaring war on the Roman state. Caesar crossed — and how we know rests entirely on people who were not standing on the bank with a stopwatch.

II

The Princes in the Tower

London · 1483

The two young sons of the late king were lodged in the Tower. Their uncle took the throne as Richard III that summer. The boys were seen less, then not at all, then never again. What happened to them — and who decided?

The accusing accounts were all written after Richard's defeat, under the dynasty that replaced him. The one writer present that summer recorded only that he did not know.

NOT-YET

A DNA test could confirm whether the bones are the princes — if permission were granted. That wall might still come down.

NEVER

Even a positive test names the bones, not the killer. Who gave the order — and how the boys died — the record cannot reach, whatever the test shows.
