However hard you pull, you never get a quark on its own — the energy you put in turns into new particles, and the stage just gets more crowded. This is real: a single free quark has never been observed. Everything we know about the inside of a proton is inferred from how it scatters when smashed — a model built entirely from the outside. The proton is a box whose lid cannot be lifted. So when the method hands us a model, this lesson's question is: is that the mechanism, or one interpretation that happens to fit?
