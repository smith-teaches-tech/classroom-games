Driving question: How do our interpretations — and our sense of a work's value — change with the context we have?

The turn — 1
In every case the object stayed exactly the same; what changed was what you knew. So did context change the artwork itself, or only your justification for valuing it? (And: for the Arnolfini, did the painting change — or did you?)
The turn — 2
The forgery's value was all origin; the urinal's object barely mattered — the idea was the work. Same question, opposite answers. So where does art-knowledge live — in the object, the maker, or the institution that frames it?
The turn — 3
van Meegeren confessed — settled. The Leonardo attribution, the Arnolfini's meaning, Fountain's author — disputed. So “context changes value” is not “anything goes.” What disciplines a reading — what would make one simply wrong?
End assignment / Choose one

Write one paragraph. You pick the question.

A · context & value
At least one rating changed when you learned a piece's context — though the object never did. Using two pieces, argue whether context changes the artwork itself or only our justification for valuing it. Make the strongest case for one side, then say what the other side gets right.
B · what makes art
Congo only painted, the Bechers only photographed what was there, Duchamp only chose. Using two of them, explain what actually confers the status ‘art’ — and what that reveals about where art-knowledge comes from. (Not whether they are good — whether they are art.)
C · perception
The Arnolfini Portrait shows that learning where to look changes what you see. Using the Arnolfini and one other piece, argue: does the knowledge a work gives you live in the work, or in what the viewer brings to it? What disciplines a ‘seeing’ so it counts as knowledge and not just projection?
D · what art-knowledge holds
A work might let you know three different things: about the world, what something is like, or about art itself. Choose one piece and argue which kind of knowledge (if any) it offers — and show how context and perception shaped your answer.

Whichever you choose: if I deleted your example, a claim about how art-knowledge works must still be standing. Write about the knowledge, not just the picture.
