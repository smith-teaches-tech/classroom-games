Where does knowledge live in a work of art?

Before we answer that, we're going to find out what you already believe about art — without telling you what you believe.

Lesson question: Where does knowledge live in a work of art — and is that the same as what makes it art, or what makes it good?

Sort 1 / Commit on paper

Nine things people say make something “art.”

ArtDoes it make the thing count as art at all? A status — in the category, or out.
GoodGranting it's art, does it make it good art — accomplished, worth your attention? A judgment of quality.
NeitherDoes neither of those.

By good we mean good as art — not morally good, and not “worth money” (that's its own card). One card can go in more than one column. Commit before we compare — there are no right answers yet, only yours.

The first cut

What were you actually measuring?

Take nice to look at and worth money. Did you call those ART, or GOOD?
Some cards confer a status — “this counts as art.” Others confer merit — “this is good as art.” Most of us sorted as if those were one question.
But an ugly painting can still be art. A beautiful sunset isn't art at all. Skill can be poured into kitsch.
Arthood and merit come apart — your sort just proved it.

The instrument

Every card you sorted was a reason.

“What counts as a good reason for calling something art?” is a real question — and it's one of four you can ask of any claim, in any subject.

The four questions  
EEvidencewhat counts as a reason here?
VValidationhow does a claim go from asserted to accepted?
CCommunitywho certifies it — and what do they certify?
RReachwhat can this kind of knowing never settle?

This grid is the instrument for the whole unit — and it travels. The same four questions get different answers in science, history, religion, maths. Watch them work where they're easiest first.

Sort 2 / The question this unit is about

Same nine cards. New question.

Which of these make a thing a source of knowledge — does it teach you, show you, let you know something?
Sort them again, on your paper.

Harder, isn't it? Art you could sort. Good you could sort. Knowledge — where does it even live in a painting?
That difficulty is the entire unit.
