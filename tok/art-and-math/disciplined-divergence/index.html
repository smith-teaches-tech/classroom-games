The move

The picture didn't change — your stance did, and the stance chose which details counted as evidence. That selecting lens has a name you already met: perspective. The text never changed; the knowledge did.

Now the names — after, not before

The power/prey read is broadly Marxist; the choices read is feminist / reader-response. You ran both without the labels. Naming them is housekeeping — the move is what you did by hand.

The discipline · why this isn't anything-goes

What stopped you inventing nonsense?

Both your lists pointed at things in the work. Neither was free invention. That's the discipline — a reading has to anchor to something the work actually offers:

1. the words / what's shown 2. the purpose 3. the rest of the work 4. precedent — prior readings 5. who decides — the gate
The cut · justified vs merely possible

A reading is justified when it's anchored. A reading is merely possible when nothing in the work reaches it — you could say it, but nothing holds it up. Several readings can be justified at once. That is not the same as all readings being legitimate. Hold the question for the next specimen: can the work carry it?

The cut · divergence is bounded

Read 2 came up empty in your own hand — the work resisted it. The reading is ruled out by the work, not by a teacher's decree. And note the cut: an unanchored reading is unanchored, not false. Ruling a reading out isn't calling it wrong — it's saying nothing holds it up. Defend it or drop it.

Specimen 2 · a second place knowledge lives

Guernica doesn't tell you war is horror.

It doesn't argue a thesis or assert a proposition. It makes you know it firsthand — the thing it shows, it shows by being it. Philosophers call this exemplification: the work doesn't point at horror, it hands you a sample of it.

Three places art-knowledge can live

① In the reading — disciplined interpretation (Specimen 1).

② In what the work shows you firsthand — exemplification / what-it's-like (this one).

③ In what the work discovers — coming next. ▸

A wrinkle · doesn't the artist decide?

Intent is an anchor — not the trump. And it drifts.

The Brillo carton from Lesson 3 was designed by James Harvey as commercial packaging. He didn't intend art; he denied his boxes were art at all. Yet the identical design, conferred by Warhol and the artworld, is art.

L3 callback

“Ask the maker” already died on Harvey. The maker's intention is one anchor among five — it can be outrun by what the work supports and what the community confers. Meaning lives in the work + a competent reading, not in the maker's say-so.

What Cage discovered (his own account)

In 1951 Cage sat in Harvard's anechoic chamber — a room built to be perfectly silent — and still heard two sounds. The engineer told him the high one was his nervous system, the low one his blood. His conclusion: there is no such thing as silence.

4′33″ doesn't interpret anything. The frame — score, performer, hush — makes you finally listen, and the room fills with sound that was always there. That's a discovery, true for every listener. You can't dissent from it the way you dissented from a Marxist Red Riding Hood. Knowledge made in the work — not in the reading.

The fill · where art-knowledge lives

One mechanism. Three behaviours.

Red Riding Hoodinterpreted
REAL divergence — several anchored readings, all justified. The lens chooses the evidence; perspective is doing real work.
Guernicaexemplified
BOUNDED divergence — some readings the work won't carry. And it shows you horror firsthand, not as a claim.
4′33″discovered
NO divergence — nothing to read; a discovery that binds every listener.

So art-knowledge isn't one thing — and it isn't all “the interpretation.” That fills the two cells we left blank since Lesson 1:

E
The work itself — what it shows, exemplifies, or discovers — disciplined by the five anchors. “Evidence” chafes: art presents, it doesn't prove.
V
A reading is accepted when it survives the anchors. That certifies it disciplined — not correct.
The based part: the “soft” AOK turns out to have a gate — and even discoveries. Not anything-goes. Next: mathematics — the AOK whose gate is the hardest there is. Same axis, opposite pole.
